Landline Nuisance Callers

Is the caller asking you for your name, Date of Birth (D.O.B), or address?

This can be a clear indication of voice phishing whereby the caller attempts to trick users into thinking they are a legitimate company to in return gain sensitive information from you!

Is the caller being impatient and demanding?

This can be a clear indication that they are trying to take advantage of you by trying to gain your sensitive data through pressure tactics.

Does the caller sound like a robot?

If so, it might be an automated call that was previously recorded using a human voice or computer. These calls can be quite convincing, but not all of them are harmless. That's why it's important to identify the number calling you and be aware of the questions and answers they are trying to retrieve from you.

PHISHING EMAIL ARTISTS

Is the email address unusual? If an email address domain consists of random characters that have no meaning, chances are it has been automatically generated and is not from a legitimate source.

Are there spelling and grammar mistakes? Organizations that are valid will not have grammatical errors in their emails. If you notice any, it could be a sign of fraud.

Is there encouragement to click on an unknown link? No email should prompt you to click on a link. Instead, hover over the link to check the URL, and ensure it directs you to an authentic website.

Is there a request for you to share personal information? It is never safe to share personal and sensitive information via email, especially when it comes to banking and identification details.
